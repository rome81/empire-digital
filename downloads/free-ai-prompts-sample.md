## Prompt 1: Client Proposal Writer

Write a proposal fast that sounds professional and wins the gig.

### Template

```
Write a professional freelance proposal for a client who needs [PROJECT DESCRIPTION]. Include: a brief understanding of their needs, my proposed approach in 3 steps, a timeline estimate, and a confident closing line. Keep it under 200 words and sound human, not robotic.
```

### Variable to fill in

[PROJECT DESCRIPTION] - Describe what the client wants done, e.g. "a redesign of their Shopify landing page to increase conversions"

### Example Output

"Hi Sarah, I completely understand the challenge -- your current landing page has strong traffic but visitors aren't converting. Here's my plan: First, I'll audit your page flow and identify the top 3 friction points. Second, I'll redesign the hero section and CTA layout using proven conversion patterns. Third, I'll A/B test two versions over 7 days so we pick the winner with real data. Timeline: 10 business days from kickoff. I've helped 6 Shopify stores increase conversions by 25%+ this year. Let's make yours next."

## Prompt 2: Blog Post Outline Generator

Generate a full blog outline in 30 seconds instead of staring at a blank page.

### Template

```
Create a detailed blog post outline for the topic: [TOPIC]. Include a compelling headline, 5-7 subheadings with 2-3 bullet points under each, a hook for the intro, and a call-to-action for the conclusion. The target audience is [AUDIENCE].
```

### Variable to fill in

[TOPIC] - Your blog subject, e.g. "how solopreneurs can automate client onboarding"

### Example Output

"Headline: Stop Doing Client Onboarding Manually -- Here's How to Automate It in a Weekend. Subheading 1: Why manual onboarding is costing you clients. Subheading 2: The 4 tools that handle 90% of the work. Subheading 3: Step-by-step setup in under 3 hours..."

## Prompt 3: Social Media Caption Creator

Create scroll-stopping captions for Instagram, LinkedIn, or X in one shot.

### Template

```
Write 3 social media captions for [PLATFORM] about [TOPIC]. Each caption should: start with a hook in the first line, provide value or a hot take in the body, and end with a clear call-to-action. Use a [TONE] tone. Keep each under 150 words.
```

### Variable to fill in

[PLATFORM] - Choose Instagram, LinkedIn, or X. [TOPIC] - e.g. "why freelancers should raise their rates this quarter"

### Example Output

"Caption 1: You're not charging too much. You're attracting the wrong clients. Here's the math most freelancers ignore: if you raise your rate by 20% and lose 1 out of 5 clients, you still earn more while working less. The clients who stay? They respect your work more. Action: Comment 'RATES' and I'll DM you my pricing calculator."

## Prompt 4: Invoice Follow-Up Email

Get paid faster with a firm but friendly follow-up.

### Template

```
Write a polite but firm follow-up email for an unpaid invoice. The invoice is [NUMBER OF DAYS] days overdue. The amount is [AMOUNT]. Keep the tone professional but make it clear I need payment soon. Include a specific next step and deadline.
```

### Variable to fill in

[NUMBER OF DAYS] - How many days late the payment is, e.g. "14"

### Example Output

"Subject: Invoice #1047 -- Quick Follow-Up. Hi Alex, I hope you're doing well. I wanted to check in on invoice #1047 for $2,400, which was due 14 days ago. I understand things get busy, but I'd appreciate if we could get this resolved by Friday, April 11th. If there's an issue with the invoice, let me know and I'm happy to sort it out. I've reattached the invoice for convenience."

## Prompt 5: Project Scope Definer

Prevent scope creep before it starts by defining clear boundaries upfront.

### Template

```
Help me define a clear project scope for [PROJECT TYPE]. Include: project objectives (3 bullet points), specific deliverables with quantities, what is explicitly NOT included, 3 milestones with dates, and acceptance criteria. Make it professional enough to include in a contract.
```

### Variable to fill in

[PROJECT TYPE] - e.g. "a brand identity package for a fitness startup"

### Example Output

"Project Objectives: 1) Create a cohesive visual identity that positions FitCore as a premium brand. 2) Deliver print-ready and digital-ready assets. 3) Complete within 3 weeks. Deliverables: Primary logo (3 concepts, 1 final), color palette (5 colors), typography guide, business card design. NOT Included: website design, social media templates, photography, print production..."

## Prompt 6: Cold Outreach Email

Land new clients without sounding desperate or spammy.

### Template

```
Write a cold outreach email to a potential client in the [INDUSTRY] industry. I offer [YOUR SERVICE]. Reference something specific about their business to show I did my research. Keep it under 100 words, include one clear value proposition, and end with a low-friction call-to-action (not "hop on a call").
```

### Variable to fill in

[INDUSTRY] - The prospect's industry, e.g. "e-commerce fashion"

### Example Output

"Subject: Quick idea for your product pages. Hi Megan, I noticed your new spring collection looks amazing, but your product descriptions might be leaving money on the table. I help e-commerce brands write product copy that converts -- my last client saw a 31% increase in add-to-cart rates after a copy refresh. Would it be helpful if I rewrote one of your product pages as a free sample? No strings attached."

## Prompt 8: Client Feedback Request

Get testimonials and reviews without feeling awkward about asking.

### Template

```
Write a short, friendly email asking a client for a testimonial after completing [PROJECT TYPE]. Make it easy for them by including 3 specific questions they can answer instead of writing from scratch. Keep it warm and low-pressure. Mention where the testimonial will be used.
```

### Variable to fill in

[PROJECT TYPE] - What you delivered, e.g. "their new website redesign"

### Example Output

"Subject: Quick favor? (takes 2 min). Hi David, now that your new site has been live for a couple weeks, I'd love to hear how it's going! If you have 2 minutes, could you answer any of these: 1) What was the biggest difference you noticed after the redesign? 2) Would you recommend working with me to other business owners? 3) Was there a specific moment where you thought 'this was worth it'? I'd love to feature your words on my portfolio (with your permission of course). A few sentences is perfect!"

## Prompt 9: Weekly Productivity Planner

Plan your entire week in 60 seconds instead of spending Monday morning in chaos.

### Template

```
I'm a freelancer working on these projects this week: [LIST PROJECTS]. I have [HOURS] available hours. Create a weekly schedule Monday-Friday that: prioritizes deep work in the morning, batches similar tasks together, includes buffer time for revisions, and blocks 30 minutes daily for business development. Format as a simple daily list.
```

### Variable to fill in

[LIST PROJECTS] - Your active projects, e.g. "website redesign for Client A (deadline Thursday), blog series for Client B (3 posts), logo concepts for Client C"

### Example Output

"Monday: 9-12 Deep work on Client A website (hero + about page). 12:30-1 Business development outreach. 1-3 Blog post #1 draft for Client B. 3-3:30 Buffer/revisions. Tuesday: 9-11:30 Client A website (product pages). 11:30-12 Business development. 1-3 Logo concept sketches for Client C. 3-4 Blog post #2 draft..."

## Prompt 10: Content Calendar Generator

Build a month of content ideas in under 2 minutes.

### Template

```
Create a 4-week social media content calendar for a [BUSINESS TYPE] business. Include 3 posts per week across [PLATFORMS]. Each entry should have: the content type (educational, promotional, personal story, engagement), a one-line post idea, and the best day/time to post. Mix content types so it doesn't feel repetitive.
```

### Variable to fill in

[BUSINESS TYPE] - Your niche, e.g. "freelance graphic design"

### Example Output

"Week 1, Monday (Educational): 'The 3-second rule: why your logo needs to work at thumbnail size -- post a side-by-side example.' Wednesday (Personal Story): 'Share a before/after of your worst early design vs. your work today -- vulnerability builds trust.' Friday (Engagement): 'Poll: Which logo style do you prefer for a coffee brand? Option A vs Option B -- drive comments.'"
